## 2022 Baseline: The State of AI Before the Explosion
===============

By 2022, AI had already made significant strides in various domains. Let's review the dominant models that set the stage for the explosion to come.

* **GPT-3**: Released in June 2021, GPT-3 marked a major milestone in natural language processing (NLP). Its impressive capabilities sparked widespread adoption and research.
* **Stable Diffusion v1**: This generative model, launched in August 2021, demonstrated remarkable image synthesis capabilities. Its success paved the way for further advancements in computer vision.

Compute constraints and data quality issues were already becoming apparent:

* **Compute limitations**: The need for powerful hardware to train and deploy AI models was growing. This would become a major challenge as AI adoption accelerated.
* **Data quality issues**: The importance of high-quality training data became increasingly clear, but the lack of standardization and annotation efforts remained a significant hurdle.

Early ethical frameworks were being adopted:

* **AI ethics initiatives**: Organizations like the IEEE Global Initiative on Ethics of Autonomous and Intelligent Systems and the AI Now Institute were establishing guidelines for responsible AI development. These efforts would continue to evolve as AI's impact grew.

These factors formed the foundation upon which AI would build its explosive growth from 2022 to 2026.

## 2023 Inflection Point: LLM Breakthroughs
As we look back on the AI evolution from 2022 to 2026, one pivotal year stands out - 2023. This was the inflection point where large language models (LLMs) made significant strides in terms of parameter count jumps and multi-modal capabilities emergence.

* **Parameter count jumps**: The GPT-4 to GPT-5 trajectory marked a notable increase in model complexity, with GPT-5 boasting over 1 billion parameters. This growth enabled more accurate and nuanced language understanding.
* **Multi-modal capabilities emergence**: In 2023, we saw the first glimpses of LLMs venturing into multi-modal realms, such as image-text fusion and audio-visual processing. These advancements paved the way for more comprehensive AI systems that can integrate diverse data sources.

Notably, open-source alternatives like Llama 3 and Mistral gained traction in 2023, offering developers and researchers a range of options to explore and build upon these breakthroughs.
